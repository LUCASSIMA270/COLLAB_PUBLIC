🏗️ The Industrial Renaissance: Fos-Berre 2030-2050
Decarbonizing France's Industrial Heartbeat

Welcome to the strategic command center for the Fos-Berre Industrial Transition. This repository contains the economic foresight study and strategic roadmap designed to transform one of Europe’s largest industrial hubs into a global model of sustainability and resilience.
🧐 The Mission

How do you transition a territory built on steel and oil into a green energy powerhouse?
Working alongside PwC and regional authorities, our team developed a comprehensive diagnostic and foresight study to:

    Map the Ecosystem: Analyzing petrochemicals, aeronautics (Airbus), and the Grand Port Maritime de Marseille (GPMM).

    Fuel the Change: Leveraging Green Hydrogen, Offshore Wind, and Solar Energy.

    Master the Transition: Balancing industrial sovereignty with 100% climate-positive growth.

📂 Strategic Roadmap (The Battle Plan)

The project is structured around a rigorous 4-month (100-day) timeline:

    Phase 1-2: The Foundation: Methodological framing and deep-dive data collection.

    Phase 3-4: The Ecosystem: Mapping value chains and conducting 15+ high-level stakeholder interviews.

    Phase 5: The Diagnostic: SWOT analysis and building the strategic "Scenario Framework".

    Phase 6: The Delivery: Final roadmap and validation workshops.

🔮 Foresight Scenarios

We don't just predict the future; we model it. Our study presents two distinct paths:

    Scenario A (Decarbonized Core): Focuses on greening legacy sectors—think Bio-Refining and Hydrogen-based Green Steel.

    Scenario B (Diversified Gateway): A strategic pivot towards Gigafactories (Batteries), Circular Economy, and E-commerce logistics.

🛠️ Key Levers for Decarbonization

The strategy uses powerful economic triggers to force change:

    Eco-Conditionality: Regional subsidies are no longer "neutral"; funding is strictly tied to green commitments.

    Industrial Symbiosis: Transforming one factory’s waste (like "fatal heat") into another’s raw material.

    Zero Smoke Stopovers: Electrifying the Port of Marseille to cut maritime emissions to zero.
